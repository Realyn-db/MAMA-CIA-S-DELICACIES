This Delicacies was started on Covid-19 pandemic, a year 2019

Mama Cia’s Delicacies started in 2019 during the COVID-19 pandemic, when Etthelindita “Mama Cia” Navarro began baking cookies and torta at home to support her family. With many people staying indoors and craving homemade food, her neighbors started ordering regularly, and soon her small kitchen became a source of comfort and affordable treats in the community.

As more customers shared her products online, Mama Cia’s Delicacies slowly grew into a recognized local brand known for its soft torta, flavorful cookies, and traditional Cebuano taste. Even after the lockdowns ended, people continued to buy her delicacies because of their quality and consistent homemade flavor, helping the business remain stable and trusted in their area.

FOUNDER OF THE MAMA CIAS DELICACIES

Etthelindita Navarro is the heart and soul behind Mama Cias Delicacies. As founder and owner, she started the business during a difficult time — driving the idea of turning her home kitchen into a source of hope and comfort for her family and community. Through her dedication to quality, her love for home-cooked traditional delicacies, and her entrepreneurial spirit, she transformed a simple home kitchen into a local brand trusted by many.

Under her guidance, Mama Cias Delicacies offers lovingly made goods that reflect both her personal care and Cebuano culinary tradition. Navarro’s focus on using good ingredients, maintaining a homemade touch, and responding to customer needs helped the brand grow steadily while staying true to its roots. She remains actively involved in daily operations, recipe development, and ensuring that every batch lives up to the standard she envisioned when she first started.

Success Story of Mama Cias Delicacies

The success of Mama Cias Delicacies began in 2020 when Etthelindita Navarro decided to turn a difficult situation into an opportunity. During the COVID-19 pandemic, she started baking cookies, torta, and other delicacies from her small home kitchen to support her family. What began as a simple way to earn extra income soon caught the attention of neighbors who loved the homemade taste and affordable price of her products. With every order she received, her confidence grew, and she realized that her passion for traditional Cebuano delicacies could become a real business.

As demand increased, Etthelindita improved her recipes, upgraded her packaging, and expanded her menu. Customers started sharing her products online, and Mama Cias Delicacies slowly reached more households, even outside her barangay. Because of her hard work, consistency, and dedication to quality, the brand became known for its freshly made delicacies that bring comfort and joy to families. Today, Mama Cias Delicacies stands as a symbol of resilience — a business born in a crisis but strengthened through passion, community support, and Etthelindita Navarro’s determination to provide the best for her customers.

Challenges Faced by the Founder — Etthelindita Navarro

Starting Mama Cias Delicacies during the COVID-19 pandemic was not easy for Etthelindita Navarro. One of her biggest challenges was the unstable supply of ingredients, as lockdowns made it difficult to buy flour, eggs, and packaging materials. She often had to adjust her schedule, find alternatives, and manage with what was available just to continue fulfilling customer orders.
